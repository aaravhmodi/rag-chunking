Retrieval-augmented generation, or RAG, combines information retrieval with text generation. Documents are split into chunks, embedded, and stored in a searchable index.

When a user asks a question, the system retrieves relevant chunks and uses them as grounding context for the language model. Chunk quality affects recall, answer completeness, and hallucination risk.

Small chunks can improve precision for factual questions. Larger chunks can preserve more context for explanation-heavy tasks, but they may also introduce noise.
